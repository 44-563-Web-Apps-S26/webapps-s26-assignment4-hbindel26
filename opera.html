Hayden Bindel Light Opera

The Sorcerer

Act II, Scene: Exterior of Sir Marmaduke's mansion

(The villagers stretch their arms, yawn, rub their eyes, and sit up.)

MEN

Why, where be oi, and what be oi a doin', A sleepin' out, just when the dews du rise?

GIRLS

Why, that's the very way your health to ruin, And don't seem quite respectable likewise!

MEN

(staring at girls) Eh, that's you! Only think o' that now!

GIRLS

(coyly) What may you be at, now? Tell me, du!

MEN

(admiringly) Eh, what a nose, And eh, what eyes, miss! Lips like a rose, And cheeks likewise, miss!

GIRLS

(coyly) Oi tell you true, Which I've never done, sir, Oi loike you As I never loiked none, sir!

ALL

Eh, but oi du loike you!
